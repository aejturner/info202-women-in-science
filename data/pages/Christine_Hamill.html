Christine Hamill

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Jump to navigation Jump to search

Christine Mary Hamill (24 July 1923 – 24 March 1956) was an English mathematician who specialized in group theory and finite geometry.

Education[edit]

Hamill attended St Paul's Girls' School and the Perse School for Girls.[1] In 1942, she won a scholarship to Newnham College, Cambridge, becoming a wrangler in 1945.[2]

Career[edit]

After receiving her Ph.D. at the University of Cambridge in 1951, she was appointed to a lectureship in the University of Sheffield. In 1954, she was appointed lecturer in the University College, Ibadan, Nigeria where she died of polio in 1956, four months before she was to have married.[1][3]
