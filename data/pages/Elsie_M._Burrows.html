Elsie M. Burrows

Elsie May Burrows (1913–1986) was an English botanist who made significant contributions[clarification needed] to British postwar phycology.[1] Her primary area of research was macroalgal ecology, focusing particularly on Fucus, a genus of brown algae, and Chlorophyta, a division of the green algae.[citation needed]

Borrows graduated with a B.Sc. from the University College in Leicester. She married in 1936 and assumed a post at Liverpool University, where she spent the remainder of her career. She subsequently received her Ph.D. in 1948.[citation needed]

A founder member of the British Phycological Society, she served as the organization's vice president from 1957 to 1958.[citation needed]

In 1951, she began collecting data fmfor her monograph, Seaweeds of the British Isles. The manuscript was completed shortly before her death and published posthumously in 1991.[citation needed] A number of specimens collected and characterized by her for the Seaweed Mapping Scheme remain in the herbarium at the Ulster Museum.[citation needed]

The standard author abbreviation Burrows is used to indicate this person as the author when citing a botanical name.[2]
