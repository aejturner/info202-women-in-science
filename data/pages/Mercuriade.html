Mercuriade

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Jump to navigation Jump to search

Mercuriade (14th-century) was an Italian physician, surgeon and medical author. She is one of the few woman physicians known from the Middle Ages.

Mercuriade was a student of the University of Salerno and belonged to the minority of female students of her time period. She was the author of the treaties De Febre Pestilenti (on Crisis in Pestilent Fever), De Curatio (The Cure of Wounds) and De Ungentis (on Ungentis).[1] Her work was included in the Collectio Salernitana.[2]

She is considered one of the "Ladies of Salerno" along with Abella, Rebecca Guarna, and Francesca de Romana who attended the medical school in Salerno from its beginning and helped usher in a "'medical renaissance'" in Europe.[3]
